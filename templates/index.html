What Are Mersenne Primes?

Mersenne primes are prime numbers of the form 2^p - 1, where p is itself a prime number. They are among the rarest and most valuable numbers in mathematics.

Mersenne Prime Formula

// Mersenne Prime: 2^p - 1
// Example: 2^3 - 1 = 8 - 1 = 7 (Prime!)
// Example: 2^5 - 1 = 32 - 1 = 31 (Prime!)
// Example: 2^7 - 1 = 128 - 1 = 127 (Prime!)

Final Prediction Algorithm

def predict_next_mersenne_exponent(index):
    # Exponential model
    exp_pred = 10**(slope * index + intercept)

    # Gap constraints
    min_gap = max(1, gap_mean - 2 * gap_std)
    max_gap = gap_mean + 2 * gap_std

    # Range calculation
    range_start = max(exp_pred - max_gap//2, last_known + min_gap)

    return (range_start, range_end)
